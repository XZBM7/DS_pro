Making Predictions

1

Input Student Data

To make a prediction, you need to provide the following information:

Academic Performance
  • First Exam Grade (G1)
  • Second Exam Grade (G2)
  • Past Failures
Study Habits
  • Weekly Study Time
  • Number of Absences
  • School Support
Important: Ensure all input values are accurate for the most reliable predictions.
2

Generate Prediction

Once you've entered all required data:

  1. Click the "Predict Final Grade" button
  2. Wait for the AI model to process your data
  3. Review the predicted final grade (G3)
  4. Save the prediction to your records

To export your prediction data:

Best Practices

For Accurate Predictions

Do These
  • Use recent and accurate student data
  • Input complete information for all fields
  • Verify exam scores before submission
  • Consider seasonal variations in performance
  • Update predictions as new data becomes available
Avoid These

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate are the predictions?
Our AI model achieves 94% prediction accuracy based on training with 600+ student records. However, accuracy may vary based on input data quality and completeness.
What data do I need to make a prediction?
You need: First exam score (G1), second exam score (G2), study time, past failures, absences, and information about school support and internet access.
Can I use this for multiple students?
Yes! You can create unlimited predictions for different students. Each prediction is saved to your account and can be managed through the "All Records" section.
How is my data protected?
We use industry-standard encryption, secure authentication, and regular security audits. Your data is never shared with third parties and is only accessible through your account.
Can I export my prediction data?
Yes! You can export all your predictions to CSV format from the "All Records" page for further analysis in external tools.
What should I do if a prediction seems inaccurate?
First, verify your input data accuracy. Consider contextual factors that might affect performance. Remember that predictions are probabilistic and should be used as guidance alongside professional judgment.
Is there a limit to how many predictions I can make?
No, there are no limits on the number of predictions you can create. Our platform is designed to support educational professionals with unlimited usage.
How often is the AI model updated?
Our models are continuously monitored and periodically retrained with new data to maintain and improve accuracy. Major updates are announced through platform notifications.
